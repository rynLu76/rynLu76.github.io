##### ECG system

Suppose the sampling frequency is 200 Hz, then Fs is equal to 100 Hz,

Given that attenuation is at 0.1 Hz, passband starts from 1 Hz to 10 Hz, the attenuation at the other side is at 60 Hz, we can get that:

Wp = [1 10] / 5000, Ws = [0.1 60] / 5000,

Since the ripple within passband from 1 Hz to 10 Hz is between 0.9 and 1, then we know that the passband ripple is at most 0.1 dB, so Rp = 0.1,

Since the stopband ripple at 0.1 Hz is at least 20 dB and at 60 Hz is at least 40 dB, Rs can not be smaller than 40 dB otherwise the condition at 60 Hz will not be satisfied, so Rs = 40 dB.

``` matlab
>> [N, Wc] = buttord([1 10]/100, [0.1 60]/100, 0.1, 40);
>> [num, den] = butter(N, Wc, 's');
>> Ht = tf(num, den);
>> [H, w] = freqs(num, den);
>> plot(w, abs(H));
```

##### 3.

With different sampling frequencies the C major scale sounds different. At 4000 Hz, the sound sounds lower,  because it is at a relatively lower frequency rate, which would produce fewer oscillations through the medium. On the contrast, at 16000 Hz, there are more oscillations occur in the medium, particles oscillate with each other more frequent and make louder and higher sound. At 8000 Hz, the sound is at a relatively smooth frequency rate, which would sound gentler compare to the sound at 4000 Hz and 16000 Hz.
